# Color Identity

A card's color identity determines which decks it's legal to include in,
based on the colors of the deck's commander. It is a broader concept than
just a card's colors.

A card's color identity includes:

- The color of any mana symbols in its **mana cost**.
- The color of any mana symbols in its **rules text** (reminder text is
  included), such as activated or triggered abilities that reference a
  color.
- The color identity of any card printed on the back of a double-faced
  card, or any other face of a multi-faced card.
- Color indicators, if present (used on cards without a colored mana
  symbol in their cost, to explicitly state their color).

A card's color identity does **not** include:

- Colors of mana that a card's abilities can produce (e.g., a colorless
  artifact that taps for any color still has a colorless identity, unless
  its own rules text otherwise includes a colored mana symbol).
- Flavor text.

## Deck-building rule

Every card in a Commander deck must have a color identity that is a
subset of the commander's color identity. For example, a mono-green
commander can only be paired with cards that are green or colorless —
no other colors are permitted, even if those cards have a colorless mana
cost but colored text.

## Common misconception

Players sometimes assume "color" and "color identity" are the same
thing. They are not — a colorless artifact can still have a colored
identity if its rules text contains a colored mana symbol anywhere,
including in an ability that isn't obviously "colored" at first glance.
