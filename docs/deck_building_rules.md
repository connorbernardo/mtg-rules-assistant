# Deck Building Rules (Commander)

Commander decks follow a distinct set of construction rules different
from standard 60-card constructed formats.

Core requirements:

- A Commander deck contains exactly **100 cards total**, including the
  commander itself.
- Commander is a **singleton** format: with the exception of basic lands,
  a deck may only include one copy of any card with a given English
  name.
- The deck must include exactly **one or two commanders** (two only if
  both have the "partner" ability, or one has "partner with" naming the
  other).
- Every card in the deck must have a color identity that is a subset of
  the commander's (or commanders' combined) color identity. See
  `color_identity.md` for how color identity is determined.
- A commander must be a legendary creature, or a card that explicitly
  states it can be your commander (such as certain planeswalkers with
  that text).

## Card legality

- Not every card printed is legal in Commander. Cards are individually
  banned by a rules committee or governing body depending on the specific
  Commander format being played, separate from the deck-building
  structure rules above.
- A card being "on-color" for a commander does not guarantee it's legal —
  it must also not be on the banned list for that particular ruleset.

## Common misconception

New players sometimes assume Commander allows duplicate copies of
powerful non-land cards the way other formats do. It does not — the
singleton restriction applies to every card except basic lands,
regardless of how weak or strong the card is.
