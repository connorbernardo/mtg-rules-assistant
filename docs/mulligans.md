# Mulligans

Commander uses the **London mulligan** rule, the same system used across
most modern constructed formats.

How it works:

- A player who is unhappy with their opening hand may mulligan: they
  shuffle their hand back into their library, then draw a new seven-card
  hand.
- Each time a player mulligans, they must put a number of cards equal to
  the total number of mulligans taken so far on the bottom of their
  library, in any order, once opening hands are finalized (i.e., after
  all players have decided whether to mulligan again).
- There is no fixed limit to how many times a player can mulligan, though
  mulliganing repeatedly leaves them with very few cards in hand.
- All players who choose to mulligan draw back up to seven cards each
  time, then put cards on the bottom afterward — they are not drawing a
  progressively smaller hand size the way older mulligan rules worked.

## Commander-specific notes

- Some Commander playgroups use a "free mulligan" or "partial paris
  mulligan" house rule for a player's first game of a session, but this
  is a casual variant, not part of the official rules.
- Commander's larger 100-card singleton deck size does not change how
  mulligans work; the process is identical to any other constructed
  format.

## Common misconception

Players sometimes think the London mulligan means you keep drawing fewer
cards each time (like "6, then 5, then 4"). That was the *old* mulligan
rule. Under London mulligans, you always draw back up to seven — the
penalty is cards put on the bottom afterward, not a smaller draw.
